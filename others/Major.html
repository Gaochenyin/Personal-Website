Mathematical Analysis

Term 1. Elementary functions (including exponentials and logarithms), limits, rates of change, the derivative and its properties, applications of the derivative and differential calculus of functions of one variable.
Term 2. The definite integral, Riemann sums, antiderivatives and integration by substitution and by parts. Area between curves, and volume by slices, washers, and shells.
Term 3. Integration rules, integrating rational functions, and improper integrals. Infinite series, convergence and divergence tests (limit comparison and alternating series tests). Divergence theorem and the theorems of Green, Gauss, and Stokes.

Geometry Algebra

Term 1. The relationship between the algebraic and geometric points of view. Linear equations, vector spaces, linear dependence, bases and coordinate systems; Linear transformations and matrices; similarity; eigenvectors and eigenvalues; diagonalization.
Term 2. Orthogonality, projections, spectral theory for symmetric matrices, the singular value decomposition, the QR decomposition, least-squares, the condition number of a matrix.

Probability Theory

Term 1. Techniques for organizing data, computing, and interpreting measures of central tendency, variability, and association. Estimation, confidence intervals, tests of hypotheses, t-tests, correlation, and regression.

Mathematical Statistics

Term 1. Statistical inference, decision theory; point and interval estimation, tests of hypotheses; Neyman-Pearson theory. Bayesian analysis; maximum likelihood, large sample theory.

Applied Regression Analysis

Term 1. Constructing features from predictors. Geometry and algebra of least squares: subspaces, projections, normal equations, orthogonality, rank deficiency. Interpreting coefficients. Collinearity. Dependence and heteroscedasticity. Fits and the hat matrix. Model diagnostics. Model selection, Cp/AIC and crossvalidation, stepwise, lasso. Multiple comparisons. ANOVA, fixed and random effects.

Computational Statistics

Term 1. Kernel methods. Generalized additive models. Kernel smoothing. Gaussian mixtures and the EM algorithm. basic sampler constructions (metropolis, Gibbs sampler, data augmentation, hybrid Monte Carlo).

Advanced Computer Language Programming (C/C++)

Term 1. Introduction to the modern programming software: object-oriented design, decomposition, encapsulation, abstraction, and testing. Emphasis is on good programming style and the built-in facilities of C/C++.

Mathematical Experiments and Mathematical Software (Matlab)

Term 1. Introduction to the engineering of computer applications in Matlab, emphasizing modern software engineering principles: decomposition, abstraction, matrix computation and visulization.
